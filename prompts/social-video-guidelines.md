# BestKarma Short-Form Video Guidelines

Use this guide when creating Reels, Shorts, TikToks, or Facebook short videos from approved BestKarma articles.

## Goal

Turn a strong article into a short, useful, highly shareable video without losing the truth of the article.

The job is not to make the most sensational clip possible.
The job is to make the most accurate clip that still earns attention.

## Brand Standard

BestKarma short-form video should feel:

- calm
- intelligent
- practical
- science-backed
- anti-hype

It should not feel:

- alarmist
- smug
- overly clinical
- bro-science
- wellness-clickbait

## Core Rule

Never make a short video more certain than the article it came from.

If the article says:

- evidence is mixed
- the effect is modest
- this applies only to some people
- more research is needed

the video must preserve that reality in simplified form.

## Recommended Video Structure

### 1. Hook

Open with:

- a surprising truth
- a mistaken belief
- a hidden risk
- an overlooked practical fix

Good examples:

- `Most people think one drink helps sleep. The evidence says otherwise.`
- `The best insomnia treatment is not a pill.`
- `Bone-density loss often starts before most women are warned about it.`

Avoid:

- `Doctors won't tell you this`
- `This changes everything`
- `You are destroying your health`
- `The cure for X`

### 2. Evidence core

Include:

- one simple mechanism
- one concrete fact or number
- one caveat when needed

### 3. Practical close

End with one realistic action.

Examples:

- ask about ApoB at your next visit
- keep naps under 25 minutes
- treat protein like a longevity habit, not a gym supplement

### 4. Brand close

Use a soft CTA:

- `Follow BestKarma for science-backed health without the hype.`
- `Save this for later.`
- `Send this to someone who needs it.`

## Platform Adaptation

Use one master script, then adapt to the platform.

### Instagram Reels

- strongest on save-worthy visual explainers
- use clean cover text
- keep caption concise and useful

### Facebook Reels

- slightly broader, more plainspoken language
- practical angles tend to travel better than niche optimization content

### TikTok

- fastest hook
- more conversational delivery
- strongest when the first line breaks a common assumption

### YouTube Shorts

- stronger search-oriented title
- slightly more explanatory than TikTok
- good fit for evergreen topics

## Health Content Safety Rules

- Do not diagnose.
- Do not promise treatment outcomes.
- Do not imply one-size-fits-all advice.
- Do not make supplement or intervention claims stronger than the article.
- Do not remove the article’s important caveat just to improve the hook.

## Preferred Angles

- counterintuitive truth
- myth vs evidence
- what actually works
- what to ask your doctor
- what to stop overvaluing
- one habit that matters more than people think

## Avoid These Angles

- miracle framing
- secret hack framing
- outrage bait
- anti-doctor framing
- fear-first framing without useful action

## Production Note

Each video package should produce:

- one master script
- one `video.json`
- one subtitle file
- one vertical video
- one cover title
- four caption variants

The source article remains the editorial truth. The short video is a distribution asset derived from it.
